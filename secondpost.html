Experiences women face in healthcare

Posted by Steven Johnson on April 28, 2023

Women in our country today face a lot of sexism especially inside of Healthcare. Over the years, women have had more chronic conditions, functional limitations, depression, and disability when compared to me. Also maternal mortality has increased in the United States over the last few decades. This is definitely a problem because a higher maternal mortality rate could actually drastically affect the population in the country if it gets too high. On top of this, women have to face more barriers when it comes to accessing healthcare which is an issue because women are more likely to need healthcare services for reproductive care and chronic conditions than men. It has also been established that a lack of power in healthcare institutions adds to the barrier that women face. These are both indirect and direct reasons like for example underrepresentation in political decision-making regarding health care and other access barriers that restrict women’s reproductive choices.

?

Here are the facts

Even when getting treated, women still face sexism and do not get the same treatment as men. For example, 10% of women report having a limited choice when it came to care while just 5% of men said the same thing. Women are also more likely to report that their provider did not spend enough time with them during their last visit than men. These are just some of the few barriers women have to face but there are plenty more when it comes to healthcare in this country..
